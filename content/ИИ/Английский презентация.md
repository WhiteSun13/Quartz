### **Slide 1: Introduction**

Hello everyone! My name is **Your Name**, and I am a software engineer. Today, I want to talk to you about a tool that is just as important to me as my laptop or my favorite programming language. I am talking about the English language. I will share how English shapes my professional life and why every developer needs it.

### **Slide 2: English as a Global Standard**

First, let’s look at the big picture. How widespread is English in the IT world? The answer is simple: it is the absolute global standard. Over 60% of all websites on the internet are in English. Almost every international tech company uses it as their corporate language. In software engineering, English is not a "foreign" language — it is our native professional language.

### **Slide 3: Why is it important in Software Engineering?**

Why is it so important? Well, programming languages themselves — like Python, Java, or C++ — are based on English. We use words like "if", "while", "print", "true", and "false". If you know basic English, you already understand the fundamental logic of the code. But of course, it goes much deeper than just the code syntax.

### **Slide 4: How I use it — Daily Coding**

Let me tell you how I use English in my daily work. First of all, I use it when I write code. There is a golden rule in IT: all variable names, functions, and comments must be written in English. Why? Because tomorrow, a developer from India, Germany, or Brazil might read my code. English ensures that my code is understandable to anyone in the world.

### **Slide 5: How I use it — Troubleshooting & Docs**

Secondly, I use English for troubleshooting. When I have a bug in my program, I always google the error in English. Platforms like Stack Overflow or GitHub have millions of solutions, and they are all discussed in English. If I try to search for a solution in Russian, I will only find about 5% of the available information. Also, all official documentation for new frameworks is originally written in English. 

### **Slide 6: Where it helps — Career & Global Market**

Where else does English help? It completely opens up the global job market. With a good level of English, you are not limited to your local companies. You can work remotely for startups in the USA, Europe, or Asia. This means better projects, diverse teams, and, let's be honest, a much higher salary. 

### **Slide 7: Where it helps — Professional Growth**

It also helps in professional development. The IT industry changes incredibly fast. The best IT conferences, webinars, and courses are in English. If you wait for a good technical book or an article to be translated into your native language, the technology might already be outdated. English gives you access to fresh knowledge immediately.

### **Slide 8: To learn or not to learn?**

This brings us to a popular question: Is it worth learning English if you just want to sit and write code? My answer is an absolute, 100% YES. Can you be a Junior developer without English? Maybe. But you will never become a Senior developer or a Team Lead if you cannot read original documentation or communicate with the global community.

### **Slide 9: Summary of the Benefits**

To sum up, learning English is the best investment you can make in your IT career. It saves your time when you look for bug fixes, it makes your code clean and professional, and it connects you with millions of developers around the world. It is the key to open source projects and remote work.

### **Slide 10: Conclusion**

In conclusion, English is not just a "bonus" skill for a programmer — it is a core competency. It helps us solve problems faster and build better software together. 

Thank you for your attention! I will be happy to answer any questions you might have.

### 💡 Советы по выступлению (для вас):
1. **Темп речи:** Не тараторьте. Говорите размеренно. Для уровня B1+ важна четкость произношения окончаний (особенно *-s* в 3 лице и *-ed* в прошедшем времени).
2. **Словарный запас:** В тексте использованы отличные ИТ-коллокации: *troubleshooting, bug fixes, core competency, golden rule, variable names*. Сделайте на них интонационный акцент.
3. **Что написать на слайдах:** Не копируйте этот текст на слайды! На слайдах должны быть только картинки, иконки и короткие тезисы. Например, на Слайде 5 можно просто написать *"Stack Overflow & GitHub"* и картинку с багом, а на Слайде 8 крупным шрифтом *"To learn or not to learn? -> YES!"*.
